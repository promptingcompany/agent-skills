# Content Generation Workflow

Use this workflow when the user wants to produce written content — blog posts, emails, social copy, docs, etc.

## Step 1 — Gather parameters

Ask the user (or infer from context):
- **Format** — blog post, tweet thread, LinkedIn post, email, product copy, etc.
- **Topic / brief** — what is the content about?
- **Tone** — professional, casual, technical, playful, etc.
- **Audience** — who is reading this?
- **Length** — approximate word count or "short / medium / long"
- **Call to action** (if applicable) — what should the reader do next?

## Step 2 — Generate a first draft

Write the content in one pass. Do not narrate what you are about to do — just write it.

## Step 3 — Apply the content checklist

Before presenting, verify:
- [ ] Opening line is strong — no "In today's world" or similar clichés
- [ ] Each paragraph has one clear idea
- [ ] Active voice throughout
- [ ] No unexplained jargon for the target audience
- [ ] CTA is clear and specific (if applicable)
- [ ] Length matches the requested format

## Step 4 — Present with options

Show the draft, then offer:
> "Want me to: (a) make it shorter, (b) adjust the tone, (c) add a different hook, or (d) produce an alternative version?"

## Format-specific notes

**Blog posts** — use H2 subheadings every 200-300 words; end with a summary or CTA.

**Tweet threads** — number each tweet (1/, 2/, …); hook in tweet 1; each tweet stands alone.

**Emails** — subject line first; keep body under 150 words unless long-form is requested; one CTA.

**Product copy** — lead with benefit, not feature; use present tense; max 2 sentences per point.
